Brooke's Adobe Illustrator Guide

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to make an sick gradient with added text over an image for accessibility purposes ...

Assembly time: there is no time limit to creating greatness!
Awesome rating: much awesome

Have you ever found a cool image that you want to add some text over, but you wouldn't be able to see it very well? Follow these instructions to learn how to add text to any cool images you might find and let your inner graphic designer shine through!

  • Things You'll Need

  • A desktop or laptop with Adobe Illustrator installed
  • A super cool image
  • The quote or text you want to overlay
  • A creative mind!

  1. Find an image online or one you have taken and the quote/text you want to use. Then open Adobe Illustrator.

    This works best if your picture is a bit busy with a lot going on, this will make for a great final product.

  2. Create a workboard that is the same size as your image or close to the same size, then copy and paste your image into AI.

    Use the shift key while you drag your image to the size you want it to be.

  3. Using the "rectangle" tool from your toolbar on the left, draw a square/rectangle over your image.

    You can make it as big as your image or use it to cover part of your image.

  4. Now, either use the search tool in the top right to search "gradient", or look at the properties panel on the right for gradient options.

    Sometimes the gradient option won't pop up so you may have to search for it.

  5. The gradient may pop up as black and white, this is where you change the colors.

    Depending on the color of your image, you may want to use a lighter or darker color for your gradient so that you are able to cover the part of the image making it difficult to read. But, be mindful to keep the opacity fairly low so that you are still able to make out some of your image.

  6. Click on the small dots below the slider to choose the color(s) you want to use for your gradient.

    This is also where you will adjust other settings to get that perfect you're looking for. Use the "opacity" tool to adjust the opacity of your gradient. You can also determine which way you would like your gradient to go using the tool that looks like an angle. For example, gradients set at 0° will run from left to right or vise versa over your image while gradients set at 90° will run from the top to bottom or vise versa over your image.

  7. When you have a gradient you are satisfied with, use the "text" tool to draw a textbox over your gradient.

    Type your quote or message in the text box and use the tools under the "characters". section on the properties tab on the right side of your screen to adjust font, size, and any other adjustments you want to make. To change the color of your text, highlight it and click on the "fill and stroke" tool on the toolbar on the left. Make sure to use a font that stands out on your gradient.

  8. Once you have made all of your adjustments to your gradient and changed the color and font of your text to stand out, click off your image, and save it to your computer or device.

    That's it! You've just created a super cool image with text that's easy to read!
